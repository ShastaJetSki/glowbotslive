How do I add a task?
Go to Tasks. Quick-add by typing in the box at the bottom of any category and pressing Enter. Or click + Add Task (top right / FAB button on mobile) for full options: notes, priority, due date, and parent task.
What do the colored left borders mean on task rows?
Red = Urgent, Orange = High, Indigo = Medium, Gray = Low. You can scan a whole list and instantly read priority without opening any task.
How do subtasks work?
When adding or editing a task, pick a Parent Task in the dropdown. The subtask appears nested under its parent with a toggle arrow. Check them off one by one — perfect for breaking projects into steps.
Can I add notes to a task?
Yes — the task modal has a Notes field. A one-line preview shows under the task title in the list. Great for part numbers, links, details, or reminders.
How do the date chips work?
In the task modal tap Today, Tomorrow, or +1 Week to set the due date instantly — no calendar picker needed.
Use the Overdue chip in the toolbar to instantly surface all tasks past their due date across every category.
What are the four goal types?
Goal — a specific achievement (run a 5K, get promoted). Vacation — a trip or travel experience. Bucket List — things to do before you die. Someday — ideas you don't want to lose but aren't ready to commit to yet.
What do the status options mean?
Dreaming — just an idea. Planning — actively researching. In Progress — you've started. Booked — confirmed (great for trips). Done — completed and archived.
Can I track the cost of a goal?
Yes — add an Estimated Cost in the goal form. It shows as a green dollar amount on the card so you can see at a glance what you're saving toward.
How do I link a goal to a category?
Use the Category dropdown. For example, link "Restore the Mustang" to your "Dad's Car" category so they live together on the dashboard.
The Someday tab is a safe place for ideas you love but aren't ready to plan. Review it monthly — you'll be surprised how many get promoted to real goals.
What shows up on the calendar?
Any task with a due date and any goal with a target date appears. Events are color-coded by category. Tasks and goals from all categories show together so you see the full picture.
How do I switch between month and week view?
Use the Month / Week toggle in the top-right of the calendar toolbar. Week view shows more events per day with less scrolling.
How does the mini calendar work?
The mini calendar in the left sidebar lets you jump to any date. Days with events show a small dot. Click a date to see its events in the Day Panel on the right. On mobile this opens as a sheet.
Set due dates on your important tasks. They'll appear on the calendar and in the Coming Up section on your dashboard — a great way to stay aware of the week ahead.
How many categories should I have?
5–10 is ideal. Think of them as the main areas of your life that you actively manage. Examples: House, My Car, Work, Health, Side Project, Hobby, Kids, Travel.
What are sections for?
Sections group categories on the Tasks page: Life (day-to-day), Work (professional), Personal (self-care), Hobbies (fun), Goals & Dreams (big picture). Assign a section when creating each category.
How do I change a category's icon or color?
Click the pencil icon on any category card on the dashboard, or go to Settings → Categories. Pick from 26 icons and 10 colors.
What happens to tasks if I delete a category?
The category is hidden but tasks remain. They'll appear as uncategorized. Best practice: reassign tasks before deleting a category.
The progress bar on each category card shows % of tasks marked Done. Satisfying to watch it fill up as you cross things off.
What is "My Day" on the dashboard?
The My Day panel shows every task due today — your morning briefing. It only appears when you have tasks due. Use it to plan your day in 30 seconds each morning.
How should I use priority levels?
Reserve Urgent for true emergencies (2–3 per week max). High = must happen this week. Medium = your default. Low = nice-to-do. If everything is urgent, nothing is.
Best way to use subtasks?
Create a parent task as the project ("Restore Engine") then subtasks for each step ("Order parts", "Drain oil", "Replace gaskets"). The parent shows a subtask count so you track progress.
Can I use this on my phone?
Fully. On mobile you get a bottom nav bar and a floating + button. Everything adapts. Add it to your home screen from your browser for an app-like experience.
Weekly review habit?
Every Sunday: open Tasks, hit the Overdue filter, clear stale items, then check the Calendar for the week ahead. 5 minutes keeps everything under control.
Dark mode toggle is the moon/sun icon in the top-right header on any page. Your preference is saved automatically.
